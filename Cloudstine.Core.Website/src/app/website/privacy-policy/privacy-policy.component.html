Cookies

   To improve site responsiveness, we may use "cookies" to collect information. Cookies are small files placed on your computer or mobile device as you visit various components of our websites. These help customize your experience by assigning each visitor a unique, random number as a User Identification (User ID).

   When your Internet browser visits a web page, a cookie with a unique number is set. This number is recorded in a small text file transmitted to your computer, stored in the cookie directory of your hard drive. The website can recognize your browser as a unique user during subsequent visits. Unless you voluntarily identify yourself, we have no way of knowing who you are, even if we assign a cookie to your computer.
